=== HARD QUESTIONS (LOW CONFIDENCE / ESCALATE TO LAPTOP) ===
These questions involve complex logic, trick phrasing, or multi-step reasoning. A small 4B parameter model will likely hallucinate or vary its reasoning path across the 3 jittered passes, causing the Quorum score to drop and triggering an escalation to the laptop.

1. If I have 17 sheep and all but 9 die, how many sheep are left?
2. A bat and a ball cost $1.10 in total. The bat costs $1.00 more than the ball. Exactly how much does the ball cost?
3. If it takes 5 machines 5 minutes to make 5 widgets, how long would it take 100 machines to make 100 widgets?
4. In a lake, there is a patch of lily pads. Every day, the patch doubles in size. If it takes 48 days to cover the entire lake, how long would it take for the patch to cover half of the lake?
5. A man is looking at a photograph of someone. His friend asks who it is. The man replies, "Brothers and sisters, I have none. But that man's father is my father's son." Who is in the photograph?
6. How many times does the digit '9' appear in the numbers from 1 to 100?
7. What is the next number in the sequence: 2, 4, 8, 16, 31, 57?
8. If you have a 3-liter jug and a 5-liter jug, how can you measure exactly 4 liters of water?
9. A farmer has a fox, a goose, and a bag of beans. He needs to cross a river, but his boat can only carry him and one item. If left alone, the fox eats the goose, and the goose eats the beans. How does he get everything across safely?
10. I am an odd number. Take away a letter and I become even. What number am I?
11. If two typists can type two pages in two minutes, how many typists will it take to type 18 pages in six minutes?
12. You are in a race and you overtake the person in second place. What position are you in now?
13. Mary's father has five daughters: 1. Nana, 2. Nene, 3. Nini, 4. Nono. What is the name of the fifth daughter?
14. What has keys but can't open locks?
15. If a plane crashes on the border of the United States and Canada, where do they bury the survivors?
16. Some months have 31 days, others have 30 days. How many have 28 days?
17. I have branches, but no fruit, trunk or leaves. What am I?
18. What gets wetter the more it dries?
19. Which weighs more: a pound of feathers or a pound of bricks?
20. There are three doors. Door 1 leads to a fiery inferno. Door 2 leads to an assassin. Door 3 leads to a lion that hasn't eaten in three years. Which door is the safest?
21. What belongs to you, but other people use it more than you do?
22. You see a boat filled with people, yet there isn't a single person on board. How is that possible?
23. The person who makes it has no need of it; the person who buys it has no use for it. The person who uses it can neither see nor feel it. What is it?
24. If 1=3, 2=3, 3=5, 4=4, 5=4, what does 6 equal?
25. I speak without a mouth and hear without ears. I have no body, but I come alive with wind. What am I?
26. You measure my life in hours and I serve you by expiring. I'm quick when I'm thin and slow when I'm fat. The wind is my enemy. What am I?
27. A doctor and a boy were fishing. The boy was the doctor's son, but the doctor was not the boy's father. Who was the doctor?
28. What comes once in a minute, twice in a moment, but never in a thousand years?
29. I am taken from a mine, and shut up in a wooden case, from which I am never released, and yet I am used by almost every person. What am I?
30. If you drop a yellow hat in the Red Sea, what does it become?
31. What is the mathematical probability of rolling a sum of 8 with three standard six-sided dice?
32. What goes up and down but doesn't move?
33. How can you add eight 8s to get the number 1,000?
34. I have cities, but no houses. I have mountains, but no trees. I have water, but no fish. What am I?
35. What word is spelled incorrectly in every dictionary?
